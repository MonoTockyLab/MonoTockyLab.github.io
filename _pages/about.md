Masahiro Ono is an integrative experimental and computational immunologist. He is the creator of **[Tocky (とき)](https://bit.ly/3wvqUsx)**, a Fluorescent Timer-based framework for analysing the temporal dynamics of cell signalling, activity, and differentiation in vivo.

His work combines molecular immunology, flow cytometry, genomics, and computational analysis to study how T-cell receptor signalling, Foxp3-associated programs, immune checkpoint pathways, and cytokine-responsive processes change over time. A central aim of his research is to establish experimentally anchored frameworks for understanding immune cell dynamics in cancer immunology and immunotherapy.

**Research areas**

- Cancer immunology
- Immunotherapy
- Integrative experimental and computational immunology
- Molecular and systems immunology
- Computational biology for cytometry and genomics

**Methods and frameworks**

- Tocky (Timer of Cell Kinetics and Activity)
- Fluorescent Timer-based analysis of immune dynamics
- Computational frameworks for cytometry and transcriptomic data
- Canonical approaches for genomic and single-cell analysis
